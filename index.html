Lab Coats and Toy Problems

Apr 27th, 2015

Someone clever once said that modern analytical philosophers would wear lab coats to work if they thought they could get away with it. I think something similar could be said about software engineers. So many of our hiring practices revolve around toy problems and algorithmic complexity — the things we’re all impressed by from our experience getting degrees (or from not getting a degree). But we’re putting on airs when we do that. What we do in our careers is much more of a skilled trade than mathematically complex or scientifically rigorous; it’s much more akin to skilled carpentry than it is to structural engineering.
